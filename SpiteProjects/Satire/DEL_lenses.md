## **4. RESULTS**

### **4.1 Baseline Dark Photon Emission**

All subjects showed measurable CFC in baseline condition (mean = 0.73 ± 0.12 AU, range 0.52-0.91 AU). This establishes that healthy human eyes emit a detectable dark photon flux under controlled conditions.

Intersubject variability (CV = 16.4%) may reflect individual differences in Φ (integrated information) or mitoclorian density.

### **4.2 Effect of Conventional Contact Lenses**

Introduction of conventional polymer contact lenses resulted in significant reduction in CFC:

- **Baseline:** 0.73 ± 0.12 AU
- **Conventional lenses:** 0.42 ± 0.09 AU
- **Mean reduction:** 42.5% ± 6.8%
- **Paired t-test:** t(11) = 8.34, p < 0.001, Cohen's d = 2.82 (very large effect)

This represents a highly statistically significant interference effect.

### **4.3 DEL-Optimized Lens Performance**

Subjects wearing prototype DEL-optimized lenses showed:

- **DEL lenses:** 0.71 ± 0.11 AU
- **vs. Baseline:** t(11) = 1.23, p = 0.24, d = 0.18 (not significant)
- **vs. Conventional:** t(11) = 7.89, p < 0.001, d = 2.87 (very large)

This demonstrates that appropriately designed materials can minimize interference with dark photon emission while maintaining optical correction.

### **4.4 Eyes Closed Condition**

With eyes closed:

- **Mean CFC:** 0.48 ± 0.13 AU
- **vs. Baseline:** t(11) = 5.67, p < 0.001, d = 2.15

The reduction with eyes closed (34%) is less severe than with conventional lenses (43%), suggesting:

1. Emission continues with eyes closed (retinal activity persists)
