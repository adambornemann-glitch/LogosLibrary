First — what are the complexity classes? Fix a Landauer-compliant machine. Define THERMALP as problems solvable with total dissipation polynomial in input length. What lives in there? Is it P? Bigger? Smaller? What if you bound dissipation to be sublinear? That forces almost every step to be reversible. Does that give you something like Bennett's reversible computation class?
Second — the space-dissipation tradeoff. Bennett showed you can make any computation reversible at the cost of extra tape. In your framework that's a formal tradeoff between thermodynamic cost and space. Can you prove that in Lean? That would be a beautiful theorem: for every ThermalTM there exists a reversible ThermalTM computing the same function with zero dissipation but polynomially more tape.
Third — your Bell formalization. If you've got 80 files handling the no-go structure of local hidden variables, and you've got a KMS-grounded deterministic machine that's deliberately agnostic about locality... can you connect them? Can you show that the TTM, precisely because it doesn't assume locality, evades the Bell constraints? That would be the 't Hooft link made formal.
