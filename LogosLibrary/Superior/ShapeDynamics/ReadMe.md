## What This Is

Shape Dynamics, as developed by Barbour, Bertotti, Gomes, Gryb, Koslowski, and
Mercati, reformulates general relativity as the evolution of spatial conformal
geometry. It trades four-dimensional spacetime diffeomorphism invariance for
three-dimensional diffeomorphism invariance plus three-dimensional conformal
(Weyl) invariance. The preferred slicing is by hypersurfaces of constant mean
extrinsic curvature (CMC). The dynamical arena is shape space — the space of
conformal three-geometries — and the evolution is generated by best matching.

Shape Dynamics has a problem it knows about and a problem it doesn't.

The **known problem** is quantization. The conformal symmetry group is better
behaved than the spacetime diffeomorphism group, but the path to quantum gravity
remains unclear.

The **unknown problem** is that CMC slicing — treated as a gauge condition — is
actually a thermodynamic equilibrium condition in disguise. Constant mean curvature
on a spatial slice is constant temperature. York time is thermodynamic time. The
Lichnerowicz equation is an equation of state. These identifications are not
metaphorical. They are consequences of reading Shape Dynamics through the lens of
Connes–Rovelli thermal time and the Tomita-Takesaki theorem.

Superior-Shape Dynamics (S-SD) makes these identifications precise and proves their
consequences:

1. **Time is entropy production.** The Schrödinger equation is the zero-temperature
   limit of entropic evolution. Planck's constant emerges from the gravitational
   self-energy cancelling against its own decoherence timescale.

2. **Geometry is correlation structure.** Spatial volume emerges from entropy
   production via Padmanabhan's dV = T·dS. The Bekenstein-Hawking entropy S = A/4
   identifies area with the number of correlations across the horizon.

3. **Measurement is one modular cycle.** The 2π-nat entropy threshold from the KMS
   periodicity of the modular automorphism group provides a sharp, non-arbitrary
   criterion for decoherence. Below threshold: coherence. Above: classical record.

4. **Quantum mechanics and classical Shape Dynamics are limits of one framework.**
   Temperature interpolates continuously between QM (T = 0) and classical spatial
   conformal evolution (T → ∞).

The four Lean files prove these claims with machine-checked algebra. This document
presents the physics and mathematics without Lean syntax.

## The Physical Picture

### The Central Puzzle

General relativity is timeless: the Wheeler-DeWitt equation HΨ = 0 admits no
preferred time parameter. Quantum mechanics requires time: the Schrödinger equation
iℏ ∂ψ/∂t = Hψ demands one. Shape Dynamics partially resolves this by identifying
York time — proportional to the mean extrinsic curvature — as a physical evolution
parameter. But York time is introduced as a gauge choice, not derived from first
principles.

S-SD completes the resolution: York time is thermodynamic time, and the Schrödinger
equation is what thermodynamic time looks like at zero temperature.

### The Interpolation

```
T = 0                                              T → ∞
Quantum Mechanics ←————————————————————→ Classical Shape Dynamics
  iℏ ∂ψ/∂t = Hψ                          Spatial conformal evolution
  Coherence preserved                      All interactions decohere
  No spatial geometry created              Geometry everywhere
  Entanglement entropy dominates           Thermal entropy dominates
  Pentagon fully inflated                  Pentagon collapsed to identity
```

The interpolation parameter is temperature. At T = 0, the only entropy production
is quantum (gravitational self-interaction), and the evolution equation is exactly
Schrödinger's. At high T, thermal entropy production overwhelms quantum coherence,
every interaction decoheres, and the system evolves classically on shape space.

The crossover occurs at T* = E_grav / k_B, where the gravitational self-energy
equals the thermal energy. Below T*: quantum-dominated. Above T*: thermally-dominated.
The quantum fraction

```
  f_q(T) = E_grav / (E_grav + k_B T)
```

declines smoothly from 1 (pure QM) through 1/2 (crossover) toward 0 (classical SD).

## File 2: ModularFlow — The 2π Quantization

### Background: The KMS Condition

For a quantum state ρ, the modular Hamiltonian is K = −ln ρ. For a thermal
(Gibbs) state at temperature T:

```
  ρ = exp(−H/T) / Z
  K = H/T + ln Z
```

The modular automorphism group acts as:

```
  σ_s(O) = exp(iKs) · O · exp(−iKs)
```

where s is the modular parameter. The KMS (Kubo-Martin-Schwinger) condition
states that this flow has period 2π in imaginary modular time:

```
  ⟨A σ_{s+2πi}(B)⟩ = ⟨σ_s(B) A⟩
```

This is not a postulate. It is a theorem of the Tomita-Takesaki theory for
faithful normal states on von Neumann algebras.

### The Measurement Threshold

One complete modular cycle produces exactly 2π nats of entropy. This number
is not a convention — it is the KMS periodicity.

The entropy threshold for decoherence:

```
  ΔS < 2π nats  →  subthreshold: coherence preserved, interaction reversible
  ΔS ≥ 2π nats  →  suprathreshold: decoherence, irreversible classical record
```

This resolves the measurement problem without a collapse postulate. There is only
entropy production. The boundary at 2π nats is sharp, exact, and derived from
modular theory rather than imposed by hand.

In bits: 2π / ln 2 ≈ 9.06 bits per measurement. This is the fundamental
information capacity of a single complete observation.

**Crucially**, the KMS condition is not applied to the measurement from outside.
It is already present on any subsystem whose reduced state is faithful and mixed —
which is precisely the state of any subsystem entangled with its environment. The
partial trace guarantees faithfulness; entanglement guarantees mixedness. The 2π

## File 3: Quintet — Energy, Information, Temperature

```
  E = I · k_B · T · ln 2
```

Five quantities — Energy, Mass (via E = mc²), Information I (in bits),

This is Landauer's principle read as an identity rather than an inequality:
a system with energy E at temperature T carries I = E/(k_B T ln 2) bits
of information. Equivalently, each bit costs k_B T ln 2 energy to erase
(the Landauer bound).

### Information Is Lorentz Invariant

Under a Lorentz boost with the Ott transformation (E → γE, T → γT):

```
  I' = γE / (k_B · γT · ln 2) = E / (k_B · T · ln 2) = I
```

Information content is a count of distinguishable microstates. Counting does not
depend on the observer's velocity. The Ott transformation preserves this; the
Landsberg transformation (T' = T) does not:

```
  Landsberg: I' = γE / (k_B · T · ln 2) = γI    ✗  (absurd)
```

A boosted observer seeing more microstates violates the principle that microstate
counting is frame-independent. This constitutes a sixth independent argument
against Landsberg, complementing the five in the relativistic temperature
formalization (Landauer bound, entropy invariance, free energy, Boltzmann
exponent, Gibbs entropy).

## Technical Notes

### Physical Constants

All four files carry G, ℏ, k_B, and c explicitly. Natural units are never
adopted. This is deliberate: the framework's content is in showing precisely
where each constant enters and where it cancels. The Schrödinger recovery
works because ℏ enters through the entropy-time conversion; hiding it in
natural units would obscure the mechanism.

### Positivity Infrastructure

Each file begins with a positivity/nonzeroness section. This is the Lean
analogue of the physicist's "assume all physical quantities are positive."
Every division is justified by a nonzeroness lemma; every inequality by
a positivity lemma. The infrastructure is tedious but essential for the
proofs to compile.

### What Is Axiomatized vs. Proved

The four S-SD files axiomatize nothing. Every theorem is proved from
definitions using Mathlib's real analysis library. The axioms live in
Split Mechanics (Tomita-Takesaki, GNS construction, partial trace properties)
and in nanothermodynamics (the data processing inequality). The S-SD files
are downstream of those axioms and contain only proved theorems.

### Proof Strategy

Most proofs follow one pattern:
1. Unfold definitions
2. Apply `field_simp` to clear denominators
3. Use `ring` or `nlinarith` or `grind` for the resulting polynomial identity

This reflects the physical content: the theorems are algebraic identities
dressed in physical language. The algebra is simple. The physics is in
recognizing which identities to prove and what they mean.
